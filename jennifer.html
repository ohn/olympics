JenniferKessy

Team USA Athlete, Volleyball

Born and raised in Orange County, California, Jennifer Kessy has always loved sports, family, and competition. Her first sport was gymnastics, which she believes has made her more explosive and graceful on the volleyball court. She then played softball and eventually soccer, before she stepped onto the volleyball court and knew she was home.

Growing up near San Clemente, she had the opportunity to play volleyball at the same beach where the pros train, inspiring her to work harder and excel in her sport.

Jennifer and her partner have played as a team in more than 100 tournaments. After narrowly missing the 2008 U.S. Olympic Team, they spent the next four years excelling in their sport, winning seven titles in 2009 alone. They are currently ranked #4 in the world and hope to bring home Olympic gold this summer.

The Olympic Tournament

Olympic beach volleyball consists of six groups of four teams (two players per team) playing a round-robin tournament. The first two teams of each group automatically move on to the next round. The two best third-place teams also move on to the next round, with the other four teams playing for the final two places.

The sixteen teams then enter into a bracket tournament, where the winning team of each match moves on toward the finals and the losing team is eliminated.

The final two teams play in the Gold Medal Match, with the winning team receiving the Olympic gold medal and the losing team receiving the silver. The two teams that made it to the final four but did not win compete in a separate match for the Bronze medal.

Volleyball Basics

A beach volleyball match consists of three sets, the first two played to 21 points and the final set played to 15 points. A team must lead by two points in order to be declared the winner.

To score a point in volleyball, a team must win a rally. A rally is when two teams vie to hit the ball back and fourth over the net. It begins when one team serves the ball over the net and ends when either team fails to return the ball over the net or hits the ball out of bounds.

Bump, Set, Spike!

Each time the ball enters a team's court, the players have a maximum of three hits to return the ball over the net. Here are some key moves to know so you can talk like a pro.

Bump is the action of passing the ball to a teammate using the forearms.

Set is the action of passing the ball to a teammate using an overhead motion with the hands.

Spike or attack is the action of sending the ball over the net to the opponent's court. Spikes usually consist of a player taking steps to approach the net, jumping, and aggressively slamming the ball to the ground on the other side of the net.

Block is the action of trying to prevent the ball from crossing the net into your court.

Dig is when a player prevents the ball from touching the court after a spike or attack, particularly a ball that is nearly touching the ground.

volleyballworkout

The key to volleyball is being explosive! These moves can help you jump higher, farther and faster.

< >

    Burpee Jump

    Stand up straight, with your arms by your sides and your feet at shoulder width. Squat down and place your palms on the floor in front of you. Kick your legs back into a push-up position, and perform one push-up. Pull your legs back into a squat, then jump as high as you can, bringing your arms over your head.

    Do 2 sets of 12 repetitions.

    Shooting
    Star Jump

    Similar to a jumping jack, but more explosive! Jump straight up and have your body for an "X." Land with your feet together.

    Repeat 25 times.

    Power Jump

    Hold a pair of 5-pound dumbbells against your sides with your palms facing in. With your right foot forward and left foot back, lower into a split squat. Push off the ground with your legs, getting as much air as you can while simultaneously switching your leg positions. Land with your left foot forward and your right foot back.

    Do 2 sets of 12 repetitions.

    Tabletop Twist

    In the tabletop position, raise your right knee to touch your left elbow by twisting your torso.

    Do 2 sets of 12 repetitions.

    Scissor Kick

    Lie on your back and place your hands, palms down, at your sides. Flex your feet and lift your right leg as high as you can. Lift your left leg four inches off the floor. Hold for 5 seconds and switch feet positions without touching the floor.

    Do 2 sets of 12 repetitions.

    Plank Tap

    Lie on your stomach and come up to the top of a push-up. Your feet should be together, with your arms slightly wider than shoulder width and straight. Touch your left hand to your right shoulder. Return your hand to the ground and touch your right hand to you left shoulder.

    Do 2 sets of 24 repetitions (12 on each side).
